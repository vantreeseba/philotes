# Happiness and Wellbeing from Social Connections — Scientific Research

## 1. PERMA Model (Seligman, 2011)

Martin Seligman's *Flourish* (2011) identifies five pillars of wellbeing:

- **P**ositive emotions
- **E**ngagement
- **R**elationships — explicitly identified as a core pillar
- **M**eaning
- **A**ccomplishment

Relationships are not just a *means* to happiness — they are *constitutive* of it. Tools that improve relationship quality directly increase flourishing on the R dimension.

---

## 2. Lyubomirsky's Happiness Research

Sonja Lyubomirsky (2007, *The How of Happiness*) identified the "happiness pie":

| Factor | Contribution |
|--------|-------------|
| Genetic set point | 50% |
| Life circumstances | 10% |
| **Intentional activity** | **40%** |

The 40% under volitional control is where relationship behaviors are most powerful:

- **Acts of kindness**: A single day of concentrated kind acts (vs. spreading them out) increased happiness significantly (Lyubomirsky et al., 2005)
- **Nurturing relationships**: Deliberate investment in relationships (quality time, expressions of gratitude) has sustained happiness effects
- **Savoring**: Reminiscing about positive shared experiences with others amplifies positive emotions

---

## 3. Gratitude in Relationships

### Key Research

- **Gratitude Letters (Seligman et al., 2005)**: Writing and delivering a gratitude letter to someone who had never been properly thanked produced the single largest positive effect of any positive psychology intervention tested — effects lasted weeks
- **Gratitude and Relationship Quality (Algoe, Haidt & Gable, 2008)**: Expressing gratitude to a relationship partner increased the recipient's relationship satisfaction and their sense of being cared for — and also improved the expresser's relationship quality
- **Algoe's "Find, Remind, Bind" Model (2012)**: Gratitude functions to find new relationship partners, remind us of the value of existing ones, and bind us more closely to them

### Practical Implications

- Build in prompts or mechanisms for expressing gratitude to people in one's network
- Help users identify who they haven't thanked recently
- Encourage notes that capture things to be grateful for about specific people

---

## 4. Loneliness Research (Cacioppo)

John Cacioppo's research (2008, *Loneliness*; multiple papers through 2019) established:

### Key Findings

- Loneliness is a "social pain" signal — evolutionarily adaptive, signals need for connection
- Chronic loneliness increases mortality risk by 26% (Holt-Lunstad et al., 2015)
- Loneliness is about **perceived** quality of connection, not quantity of contacts
- The US, UK, and Australia declared "loneliness epidemics" in 2017–2018
- Digital contact (social media scrolling) can paradoxically increase loneliness — passive consumption vs. active connection

### Practical Implications

- Help users identify who they feel genuinely close to vs. who is merely a contact
- Prompt deeper engagement rather than just contact-counting
- The distinction between "seen someone" and "really connected" matters

---

## 5. Acts of Kindness

### Research

- Otake et al. (2006): Simply counting acts of kindness for one week increased happiness
- Nelson et al. (2016): Performing acts of kindness for others was more effective at increasing wellbeing than self-focused activities
- **Social spending**: Dunn, Aknin & Norton (2008) found spending money on others (vs. self) predicted greater happiness — effect held across 136 countries (Aknin et al., 2013)

### Practical Implications

- Prompt users to consider what they could do for someone in their network (birthday gift ideas, favors, introductions)
- Track gestures and acts of kindness given and received as relationship data

---

## 6. The Value of Milestones and Dates

### Research

- **Temporal Landmarks (Dai et al., 2014)**: People are more receptive to fresh-start thinking around significant dates — the "fresh start effect"
- **Milestone Recognition (Amabile & Kramer, 2011)**: Acknowledging progress and milestones significantly boosts positive emotion and relationship quality
- Anniversaries of shared experiences are powerful reconnection triggers

### Practical Implications

- Surface birthdays/anniversaries in advance (not just on the day) to enable thoughtful responses
- Track personal milestones beyond birthdays (job changes, moves, graduations)

---

## 7. Key Cross-Cutting Insights

1. **Gratitude is the highest-leverage intervention** — The gratitude letter has the largest effect size of any positive psychology intervention
2. **Kindness boosts both giver and receiver** — Acts of kindness improve wellbeing for the person performing them, not just recipients
3. **Perceived quality matters more than quantity** — Loneliness is about feeling disconnected, not about contact count
4. **40% is malleable** — Nearly half of our happiness is under behavioral control; relationship behaviors are among the most effective levers
